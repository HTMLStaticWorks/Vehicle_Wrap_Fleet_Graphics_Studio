Wrap Knowledge Base

Everything you need to understand regarding wrapping lifespan, maintenance, warranties, and billing schedules.

Under standard road operations and matching our care procedures, our premium cast vehicle wraps (using 3M or Avery Dennison films) last between 5 to 7 years. Reflective chevron films and printed perforated graphics have a standard lifecycle of 3 to 5 years.

Physical wrapping requires 2 to 4 days depending on vehicle size and contours. For instance, spot decals can be applied in under a day, a standard cargo delivery van wrap takes 2 days, and large semi sleeper cab graphics require 3 to 4 days including post-cure inspections.

No. When applied onto healthy, cured OEM factory paint, premium cast vinyl wraps protect the underlying paint coat from small gravel chips and UV fading. During removal, the wrap peels off cleanly without leaving glue residue or lifting paint panels.

As certified 3M Graphics Preferred Installers, our wraps are covered by the 3M MCS Warranty program, protecting materials against visual degradation, cracking, or peeling for up to 5-7 years, supplemented by our studio's 1-year application guarantee.

We recommend hand-washing wrapped vehicles weekly using a mild car soap and soft micro-fiber towels. If using pressure washing, limit nozzle force below 2000 PSI, hold the spray head at least 12 inches away, and avoid pointing water jets directly at vinyl seams.

We work closely with fleet managers to organize batch delivery sequences. To avoid disrupting your transit routes, we wrap in cohorts of 2-5 vehicles during weekends or off-peak periods, returning finished vehicles before starting the next cohort.

Pricing is determined by vehicle square footage, contours complexity, wrap coverage (full wrap, partial, spot decals), and material types (gloss, matte, reflective, chrome). Fleet discounts are applied starting at 3 matching vehicle systems.
