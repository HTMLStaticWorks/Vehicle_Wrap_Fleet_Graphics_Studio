Our Purpose

Uncompromising Quality in Vehicle Branding

Founded in 2011, Velocibrand has grown from a single bay wrapping workshop into a nationally recognized powerhouse for fleet branding solutions. We believe that your corporate vehicle fleet is your most valuable physical marketing asset.

Our mission is to empower logistics networks, local service brands, and corporate fleets with wraps that are engineered for durability, designed for instant visual recognition, and installed to flawless, paint-like perfection.

Vision

To set the national standard for transport media graphics and engineering.

Values

Absolute precision, extreme material durability, and design integrity.

Why Velocibrand

Engineered to Perform

We go beyond styling, providing enterprise infrastructure built to handle rollouts and minimize fleet downtime.

Premium Warranties

As certified installers, we offer material warranties that guarantee wraps against fading, peeling, and lifting for up to 7 years.

Reduced Downtime

Our high-capacity detailing facility and streamlined installation processes get your commercial assets back on the road rapidly.

Design Precision

We use accurate 3D vehicle template models to construct designs, eliminating layout errors and visual distortion during wraps.

Our Journey

Timeline of Innovation

How we evolved from a regional wrapping studio to a trusted nationwide fleet branding authority.

1

2011: The Founding

Velocibrand opens its first single-bay install studio in Detroit, Michigan, focusing on premium owner-operator truck wraps and custom vehicle graphics.

2

2016: Facility Expansion

We expand our footprint to a 15,000 square-foot, climate-controlled, dust-free facility, permitting simultaneous wraps for up to six commercial transport trailers.

3

2020: National Programs

Launched the national fleet rollout program, coordinating branding wraps across multi-location networks for franchise and logistics corporations.

4

2026: 3D Visualization Studio

Introduced full 3D interactive wrap preview software, allowing client project teams to inspect layout proportions prior to wide-format printing.
